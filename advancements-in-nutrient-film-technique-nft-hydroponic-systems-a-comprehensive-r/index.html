Advancements in Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) Hydroponic Systems: A Comprehensive Review and Bibliometric Analysis

25 May 2026 4 min read
Soru & Cevap Destek ol

Authors: Onofrio Davide Palmitessa, Angelo Signore, Pietro Santamaria — University of Bari "Aldo Moro", Italy

Journal: Frontiers in Plant Science, 2024 Dec 18; 15:1504792. doi: 10.3389/fpls.2024.1504792

Source: PMC11688204 (Open Access)

Abstract

In the context of climate change, reducing the environmental impact of agriculture has become increasingly critical. The Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) is a hydroponic system designed to optimize water and nutrient use, making it a valuable tool for sustainable agriculture. This bibliometric review examines the evolution of NFT research from 1977 to 2023, analyzing 774 scientific documents. The review highlights an upward trend in NFT-related studies, with a shift from conference proceedings to peer-reviewed journal articles. Lettuce and tomatoes have emerged as the primary crops studied in NFT systems. Key areas include nitrate accumulation, biofortification, water salinity effects, and integration with aquaponics.

Keywords: water use efficiency, soilless cultivation, nutrient solution management, climate change, environmental impact, NFT

1. Introduction

Climate change is significantly affecting agricultural activities by altering weather patterns, increasing the frequency of extreme events, and shifting growing seasons. Traditional farming is particularly vulnerable, as it relies heavily on stable environmental conditions. Innovative agricultural practices such as greenhouse farming and soilless cultivation (hydroponics and aeroponics) offer promising solutions.

The Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) delivers nutrient solution directly to plant roots, eliminating the need for growing media and reducing water and fertilizer usage. The NFT system was developed at the Glasshouse Crops Research Institute (Littlehampton, England) in the late 1960s. Unlike conventional farming, NFT does not require soil, eliminating soil-related issues such as erosion, compaction, and nutrient depletion. It uses significantly less water compared to traditional farming methods, as water is recirculated within the system.

2. Research Trends (1977–2023)

The production of scientific papers on NFT reached a peak of 81 in 2023, with a steady increase that became more stable from 2013 onwards. Until the early 1990s, only a few papers were published on the NFT topic; from 1991 onwards scientific productivity steadily increased, with peaks in 1998, 2001, and 2004 (27, 25, and 34 papers respectively). Comparing 2022 and 2023 with 2021, publications were 50% higher in 2022 and doubled in 2023.

In 2023, approximately 5% of publications on soilless cultivation concern the NFT technique, up from 1% in the 1990–1999 period. The leading journals are Acta Horticulturae (94 papers), followed by HortScience, Journal of Plant Nutrition, and Scientia Horticulturae. Brazil and the USA lead in publications with 106 each, followed by Japan, Italy, India, Poland, Egypt, and Greece.

The most common research topics: hydroponics (26% of publications), NFT (24%), lettuce (11%), tomato (6%), salinity (5%), greenhouse and aquaponics (3% each).

3. Key Issues in NFT Systems

3.1 Salinity Tolerance

Salinity stress is a significant constraint to plant growth in NFT systems, where plants are directly exposed to saline nutrient solutions and salinity can build up in the recirculating solution due to water evaporation and selective nutrient absorption. Mitigation strategies include: selection of salt-tolerant cultivars, grafting salt-sensitive cultivars onto salt-tolerant rootstocks, adjusting nutrient solution composition (increasing potassium levels), use of biostimulants such as glycinebetaine, and advanced sensor-based monitoring and control systems.

3.2 Nutrient Management

Maintaining optimal nutrient levels is crucial. Different plant species and growth stages have varying nutrient requirements. The uptake of one nutrient can be influenced by others — for example, high potassium levels can inhibit magnesium uptake. Sensor-based nutrient monitoring and precision fertigation techniques are key innovations for optimizing nutrient management.

3.3 Oxygen Availability

Adequate oxygen supply to roots is essential. In NFT systems, the oxygen content of the nutrient solution can fluctuate due to temperature, plant respiration, and microbial activity. High plant density increases oxygen demand; elevated temperatures reduce oxygen solubility. Innovations like the Nutrient Drip Technique (NDT) and the New Growing System (NGS) have shown promise in maintaining higher dissolved oxygen levels.

4. Emerging Challenges

4.1 Climate Change

Rising temperatures, changes in precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events can all impact NFT production. Water scarcity, heat stress on plants, and increased pest and disease pressure are key concerns. Higher temperatures reduce oxygen solubility and accelerate evaporation from the nutrient solution.

4.2 Microbial Contamination

NFT systems provide a favorable environment for microorganism growth. The warm, humid conditions and nutrient-rich solution facilitate rapid microbial growth. Eliminating soil (a common reservoir for zoosporic pathogens) reduces infection risk, but strict sanitation and regular water quality monitoring are essential.

4.3 Sustainability

Water usage, energy consumption, and waste management are key sustainability concerns. NFT systems use about 10% of the water compared to open-field-grown lettuce. Energy consumption for pumps, aeration systems, and — in water-scarce regions — desalination poses challenges. Using renewable energy sources could significantly reduce emissions.

5. Opportunities

5.1 Vertical Farming

NFT systems are well-suited for vertical farming due to their ability to grow crops in limited space without soil. Benefits include higher yields, reduced water usage, year-round production independent of weather, and reduced transportation costs in urban settings.

5.2 Biofortification

NFT's precise control over nutrient delivery makes it ideal for biofortification — enhancing the nutritional value of crops. Studies have demonstrated successful iodine, selenium, and iron biofortification in lettuce using NFT systems.

5.3 Aquaponics Integration

Combining NFT with aquaponics creates a closed-loop system where fish waste provides nutrients for plants and plants purify water for fish. This integration achieves efficient nutrient delivery, enhanced system resilience, and optimized resource utilization. The optimal fish-to-plant ratio is approximately 15–42 grams of fish feed per m² of plant growing area.

6. Conclusion

This bibliometric review of 774 scientific documents on NFT highlights the evolution and increasing interest in this cultivation method from 1977 to 2023. Lettuce and tomato have been the primary focus, reflecting NFT's broad applicability. Research on lettuce has concentrated on nitrate accumulation and biofortification; studies on tomatoes have highlighted oxygen concentration challenges.

Continued research and innovation are essential to address existing challenges and harness the full potential of NFT for sustainable agriculture. Future studies should focus on refining nutrient delivery systems, enhancing environmental control, and exploring new crop varieties.
